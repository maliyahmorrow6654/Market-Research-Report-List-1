<p><p>Sodium silicate powder, also known as water glass or liquid glass, is a versatile chemical compound formed by combining sodium oxide (Na2O) with silica (SiO2) at high temperatures. Sodium silicate powder finds applications in various industries, including adhesives, detergents, construction, ceramics, and healthcare, owing to its excellent binding, adhesive, and protective properties.</p><p>The market analysis of sodium silicate powder suggests significant growth opportunities for the industry in the coming years. <p><ul><li>Hydrous</li><li>Anhydrous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sodium Silicate Powder is available in two primary types: Hydrous and Anhydrous. Hydrous sodium silicate powder is composed of water and sodium silicate, offering a lower concentration of silica. It is commonly used in adhesives, detergents, and construction materials. Anhydrous sodium silicate powder, on the other hand, has a higher concentration of silica as it lacks water. It finds applications in catalyst manufacturing, foundries, and water treatment. Both types have distinct applications based on their silica concentration and presence of water in their composition.</p></p>
