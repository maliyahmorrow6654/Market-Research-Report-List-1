<p><p>Automotive seat belt pretensioners refer to safety devices designed to reduce movement and ensure maximum protection in the event of a collision. Two common types are anchor and buckle pretensioners, as well as seat belt retractor pretensioners. Anchor and buckle pretensioners work by tightening the seat belt at the anchor point and buckle, respectively, upon impact, reducing slack and securing occupants in their seat. On the other hand, seat belt retractor pretensioners retract excess seat belt webbing immediately, restraining occupants more effectively and minimizing potential injuries. These technologies enhance overall safety in vehicles.</p></p>
