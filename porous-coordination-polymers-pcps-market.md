<p><p>Porous Coordination Polymers (PCPs) are a class of materials with a crystalline structure that contains regular pores or cavities. These materials have various applications in fields like gas storage, separation, catalysis, and sensing. The PCP market is categorized into different types based on the metal ion used in their construction. Zinc-based PCPs are widely used and have high stability. Copper-based PCPs exhibit good electrical conductivity. Iron-based PCPs have magnetic properties. Aluminum-based PCPs show excellent thermal stability. Magnesium-based PCPs offer exceptional adsorption capacity. Lastly, other types of PCPs include those constructed using different metal ions or combination of metals.</p></p>
