<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Ambulance vehicles can be classified into four types based on their construction and purposes. Type I ambulances are mounted on a truck chassis and are mainly used for transporting patients with critical medical conditions to hospitals. Type II ambulances are designed for non-emergency medical transportation and are typically van-based. Type III ambulances are built on a cutaway van or truck chassis, featuring modular construction, and are used for transporting patients who do not require advanced life support. Type IV ambulances are smaller vehicles often used for transporting patients in congested areas or for non-emergency medical transfer.</p></p>
