<p><p>The automotive HVAC heat exchanger market comprises three main types: condensers, evaporators, and heat cores. Condensers are responsible for converting high-pressure refrigerant gas into a liquid state by releasing heat. Evaporators, on the other hand, absorb heat and humidity from the vehicle's interior, turning liquid refrigerant into gas in the process. Heat cores are used in vehicles with heat recovery systems, where they transfer waste heat from the engine to warm the cabin. These components play essential roles in maintaining comfortable temperature and climate inside vehicles.</p></p>
