<p><p>Bionematicides are a type of pesticides designed to control nematode populations in agriculture. There are two main types of bionematicides available in the market: microbials and biochemicals. Microbials are products that contain live microorganisms such as bacteria or fungi that can suppress nematode populations. On the other hand, biochemicals are derived from natural substances and work by disrupting nematode biochemical processes. Both types offer environmentally friendly alternatives to synthetic chemical nematicides and are increasingly being adopted by farmers to reduce the impact of nematode damage on crops.</p></p>
