<p><p>Shoe insoles, also known as shoe inserts or orthotics, are cushioned or supportive devices that are placed inside footwear to provide additional comfort, support, and stability to the feet. This, coupled with the rising prevalence of foot-related disorders, is driving the demand for shoe insoles.</p><p>Additionally, the growing popularity of athletic activities and fitness trends is also contributing to the market growth. Athletes and fitness enthusiasts often require specialized footwear and insoles to enhance their performance and prevent injuries. The advancements in technology have led to the development of innovative and technologically advanced insoles, further fueling market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing geriatric population and the rising adoption of footwear with enhanced comfort features among the elderly are anticipated to drive the demand for shoe insoles.
