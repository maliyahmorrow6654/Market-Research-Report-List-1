<p><p>The synthetic dye and pigment market is categorized into various types based on their chemical properties and applications. Acidic dyes are water-soluble and primarily used for dyeing wool, silk, and nylon. Basic dyes are also water-soluble but are mainly used for dyeing acrylic and cationic fibers. Direct dyes are versatile and can dye a wide range of materials, including cotton and rayon. Reactive dyes form covalent bonds with fibers, providing excellent colorfastness. Solvent dyes are used for coloring non-polar materials like plastics and hydrocarbon fuels. Disperse dyes are used for coloring synthetic fibers like polyester. Other types include vat dyes, sulfur dyes, and metal complex dyes, among others.</p></p>
