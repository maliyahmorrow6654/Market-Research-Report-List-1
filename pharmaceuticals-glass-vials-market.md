<p><p>The pharmaceuticals glass vials market can be classified into three types: Type I glass, Type II glass, and Type III glass. Type I glass is made of borosilicate and highly resistant to chemical changes, making it suitable for storing drugs, vaccines, and biologicals. Type II glass, also known as treated soda-lime-silica glass, offers good chemical resistance and is commonly used for non-aqueous liquid medications. Type III glass, made of regular soda-lime-silica glass, is less resistant to chemical changes and is typically used for packaging solid oral medications. These categorizations help ensure the appropriate containment and preservation of pharmaceutical products.</p></p>
