<p><p>A biodegradable Mg-Alloy implant refers to a type of medical implant made from magnesium (Mg) and aluminum (Al) alloy that is designed to degrade over time once implanted in the human body. As the demand for minimally invasive procedures and personalized medicine increases, the need for biodegradable implants is also expected to rise. These implants offer several advantages over traditional permanent implants, such as reduced risk of infection, elimination of the need for secondary surgery to remove the implant, and better compatibility with human tissues.</p><p>Additionally, increasing advancements in material science and manufacturing technologies are expected to drive the market growth. Researchers are continuously exploring new alloy compositions and fabrication techniques to enhance the biodegradation rate and mechanical properties of these implants. However, challenges related to the stability and corrosion resistance of Mg-Alloy implants, as well as the stringent regulatory approval process, may hinder market expansion to some extent.</p></p>
