<p><p>The liquid desiccant market encompasses two main types: organic solvent and inorganic solvent. Organic solvent liquid desiccants are made from organic compounds, such as amines or natural oils. They are commonly used in applications that require a higher moisture removal capacity, such as HVAC systems and industrial dehumidifiers. In contrast, inorganic solvent liquid desiccants are derived from inorganic compounds like lithium chloride or calcium chloride. They are typically used in smaller-scale applications, like household dehumidifiers or air conditioning units, due to their lower moisture removal capacity.</p></p>
