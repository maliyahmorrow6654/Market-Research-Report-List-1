<p><p>G-line photoresists are commonly used in semiconductor manufacturing to transfer patterns onto silicon wafers. Photopolymerization photoresists involve the polymerization of monomers when exposed to ultraviolet light, forming a solid polymer image. Light decomposition type photoresists rely on the decomposition of light-sensitive compounds, resulting in the creation of a pattern. Photocrosslinked photoresists utilize a process where the materials change their physical properties upon exposure to light, enabling the formation of patterns. These different market types offer various benefits and applications, catering to the diverse needs of the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
