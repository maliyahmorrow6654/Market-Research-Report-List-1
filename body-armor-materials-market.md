<p><p>The body armor materials market is categorized into four types: metals & alloys, ceramics, composites fiber, and others. Metals & alloys, such as steel and titanium, offer high strength and durability but are heavy. Ceramics, like boron carbide and alumina, are lightweight and provide excellent shock absorption. Composites fiber materials combine different fibers, like aramid and polyethylene, to achieve a balance of strength and weight. The "others" category includes materials such as glass fibers and carbon nanotubes, which offer unique properties but are not as commonly used in body armor applications.</p></p>
