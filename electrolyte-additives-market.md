<p><p>Electrolyte additives play a crucial role in improving the performance and lifespan of batteries. Vinylene Carbonate (VC) is commonly used to enhance the stability of electrolyte solutions, while Fluoroethylene Carbonate (FEC) helps in forming a protective layer on the battery's electrode surface. 1,3-Propane sultone is utilized as a stabilizer and electrolyte solvent. Lithium Bis(fluorosulfonyl)imide (LiFSI) acts as a conducting salt and supports the flow of ions within the battery. Other types of additives may include flame retardants, plasticizers, and conductive agents, all aimed at enhancing the battery's efficiency and safety.</p></p>
