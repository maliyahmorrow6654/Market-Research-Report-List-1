<p><p>Automotive air suspension refers to the suspension system in vehicles that uses air-filled bellows to support the vehicle's weight and provide a more comfortable ride. There are two types of automotive air suspension: non-electronically controlled and electronically controlled. Non-electronically controlled air suspension systems use mechanical valves and components to regulate the air pressure and adjust the vehicle's height. In contrast, electronically controlled air suspension systems utilize electronic sensors, control modules, and air compressors to provide more precise control over the suspension, allowing for automatic adjustments based on driving conditions and desired ride comfort.</p></p>
