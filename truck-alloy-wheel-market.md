<p><p>The truck alloy wheel market consists of three types: casting, forging, and other. Casting involves pouring molten alloy into a mold to shape the wheel. Forging, on the other hand, uses intense heat and pressure to shape the alloy into a wheel. The "other" category includes various manufacturing techniques such as spinning, which involves spinning a disc of alloy to form a wheel shape. Each method has its own advantages and disadvantages in terms of cost, strength, and weight, influencing their application in the truck alloy wheel market.</p></p>
