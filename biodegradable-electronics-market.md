<p><p>The biodegradable electronics market can be divided into four main types: conductors, dielectric, luminescent, and electrochromic. Conductors are materials that allow the flow of electrical current, such as metals or conducting polymers. Dielectric materials, on the other hand, are non-conductive substances used to separate conductors and store electrical charge. Luminescent materials emit light when excited by an electrical current, commonly found in displays or sensors. Lastly, electrochromic materials change their color or transparency when an electric field is applied, often used in smart windows or displays.</p></p>
