<p><p>The bio-based lysine market consists of two types: crop raw materials and biofermentation market. In the crop raw materials market, lysine is obtained from various crops such as corn, wheat, and rice through extraction or enzymatic processes. On the other hand, the biofermentation market involves the production of lysine through a fermentation process, where specific microorganisms, such as Corynebacterium glutamicum, are used to convert glucose or molasses into lysine. Both these methods contribute to the production of bio-based lysine, which finds applications in animal feed, pharmaceuticals, and food industry.</p></p>
