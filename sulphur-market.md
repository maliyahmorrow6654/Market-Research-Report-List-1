<p><p>Sulphur is a non-metallic chemical element represented by the symbol S and atomic number 16. It is an abundant element and is typically found in nature as a mineral deposit. The agriculture sector is the major consumer of sulphur, primarily due to its extensive use in the production of fertilizers. Sulphur is an essential nutrient for plants and helps in improving plant growth and yield. The increasing demand for food globally, coupled with the growing emphasis on agricultural efficiency, fuels the demand for sulphur in the agriculture industry.</p><p>Furthermore, the chemical industry also contributes significantly to the sulphur market. Sulphur is used as a raw material in the production of various chemicals, including sulfuric acid, detergents, and rubber processing chemicals. The expanding industrial activities and urbanization in developing economies have boosted the demand for these chemical products, consequently driving the sulphur market.</p><p>In recent years, sustainable agriculture practices and environmental concerns have led to the adoption of sulphur in organic farming. Sulphur is used as a fungicide and pesticide in organic agriculture, offering a natural and eco-friendly alternative to synthetic chemicals. This has opened new market opportunities for sulphur, especially in regions with a growing organic farming sector.</p><p>Overall, the sulphur market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 3.20% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing demand from the agriculture and chemical sectors.
