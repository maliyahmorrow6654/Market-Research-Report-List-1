<p><p>The automotive Noise, Vibration, and Harness (NVH) materials market mainly consists of three types: rubbers, thermoplastic polymers, and engineering resins. Rubbers are elastic materials that absorb and dampen noise and vibrations, making them suitable for reducing noise in a vehicle's interior. Thermoplastic polymers offer better sound insulation and vibration reduction properties compared to rubbers. Engineering resins are lightweight and help in reducing weight and improving fuel efficiency. These materials are used in various automotive components like engine mounts, dashboards, door panels, and floor systems to enhance the overall driving experience.</p></p>
