<p><p>Elastomeric impression materials are widely used in dentistry for making accurate dental impressions. There are four main types: addition silicone, condensation silicone, polyether, and polysulfide, with each having unique properties and applications. Addition silicone is the most popular due to its excellent accuracy, dimensional stability, and easy handling. Condensation silicone is cost-effective but can shrink over time. Polyether is known for its high tear strength and good detail reproduction. Polysulfide is favored for its ability to produce highly accurate impressions in moist environments. Other types, although less common, offer their own advantages and are used in specific situations.</p></p>
