<p><p>Self-lubricated bearings are designed to reduce friction and wear between moving parts. Metallic self-lubricated bearings are made from metal alloys with built-in lubricants that are released during operation, eliminating the need for external lubrication. Non-metallic self-lubricated bearings, on the other hand, are made from polymers or composites that have self-lubricating properties. These bearings also release lubricants during use, allowing for smooth and efficient operation without the need for additional lubrication. Both types of self-lubricated bearings offer cost-effective and low-maintenance solutions for various industrial applications.</p></p>
