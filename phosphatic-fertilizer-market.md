<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthesis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Phosphatic fertilizer, commonly used in agriculture, can be categorized into two main types: natural and synthesis. Natural phosphatic fertilizers are derived from naturally occurring sources, such as bones, rock phosphate, and guano. These sources are processed to obtain phosphorus-rich products. On the other hand, synthetic phosphatic fertilizers are manufactured through chemical processes using phosphate rock. These synthetic fertilizers offer a more controlled nutrient composition and are often more readily available for plant uptake. Both types of phosphatic fertilizers play a crucial role in improving soil fertility and promoting plant growth.</p></p>
