<p><p>Geothermal heat pump systems are categorized into three main types: underground tube ground source heat pump system, groundwater source heat pump system, and surface water ground source heat pump system. In an underground tube system, pipes are buried underground to circulate a heat transfer fluid, absorbing heat from the earth. Groundwater source systems use water from underground sources to extract heat, while surface water systems utilize water bodies like lakes or rivers to transfer heat. All these systems harness the earth's natural heat to provide energy-efficient heating and cooling solutions for buildings.</p></p>
