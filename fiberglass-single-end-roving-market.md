<p><p>The Fiberglass Single-end Roving market consists of two main types: Continuous Single End Roving and Chopped Single End Roving. Continuous Single End Roving is a type of fiberglass roving that is produced in a continuous length without any cutting or chopping. It is typically used in applications that require continuous filament reinforcement, such as pultrusion or filament winding. Chopped Single End Roving, on the other hand, is a fiberglass roving that is cut into shorter lengths. It is commonly used in applications where dispersed reinforcement is needed, such as spray-up or open molding processes.</p></p>
