<p><p>The rubber anti-tack agent market can be categorized into various types based on their composition. Stearates refer to compounds that contain stearic acid and metal salts, which act as an anti-adhesive for rubber surfaces. Fatty acid esters are formed by the reaction between fatty acids and alcohols, offering lubrication and preventing rubber sticking. Fatty acid amides are formed by reacting fatty acids with amines, reducing adhesion. Soaps are composed of fatty acids and alkali metal salt, functioning as lubricants. Other types include various additives and chemicals that provide anti-tack properties to rubber surfaces.</p></p>
