<p><p>Standard PCR instruments are categorized into three market types based on the volume of samples they can process: Low Throughput, Medium Throughput, and High Throughput. Low Throughput instruments are designed for smaller laboratories or research facilities that deal with a limited number of samples per day. Medium Throughput instruments are suitable for intermediate-sized labs with a moderate workload. High Throughput instruments, on the other hand, are built for large laboratories or diagnostic centers that handle a high volume of samples regularly. These distinctions ensure that PCR instruments cater to varying needs of different laboratory settings.</p></p>
