<p><p>Degradable resin is a type of polymer that can break down into smaller molecules under specific conditions, offering an environmentally friendly alternative to traditional plastics. PLA (polylactic acid) is one such resin derived from renewable resources like cornstarch and sugarcane. PBS (polybutylene succinate) is another degradable resin that degrades in industrial composting systems. PBTA (polybutylene terephthalate adipate) is a resin used for various applications due to its biodegradable properties. Other degradable resins include PHA (polyhydroxyalkanoates) and PCL (polycaprolactone), each with unique properties suitable for specific applications.</p></p>
