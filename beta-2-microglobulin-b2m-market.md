This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of kidney diseases, such as chronic kidney disease and end-stage renal disease, is driving the demand for B2M testing for early diagnosis and monitoring of these conditions. Additionally, the rising incidence of multiple myeloma, a cancer of plasma cells, is also boosting the demand for B2M testing as it is considered a reliable marker for disease progression and response to treatment.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in technology and laboratory diagnostics have made B2M testing more accessible and accurate, further contributing to market growth.
