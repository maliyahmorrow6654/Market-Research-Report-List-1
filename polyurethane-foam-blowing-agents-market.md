<p><p>Polyurethane foam blowing agents are substances used in the manufacturing process of polyurethane foam. They help create the foam's cellular structure by expanding and forming gas bubbles within the material. There are several types of blowing agents available in the market, including hydrocarbons (HC), hydrofluorocarbons (HFC), hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFC), and hydrofluorocarbon (HFO). Each type has unique properties and environmental impacts. HCFCs and HFCs have ozone-depleting potential, while HFOs offer a more eco-friendly alternative due to their lower global warming potential. The choice of blowing agent depends on factors such as desired foam properties, cost, and environmental considerations.</p></p>
